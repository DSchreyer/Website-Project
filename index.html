About Me!

I am a passionate Bioinformatician with interest in oncology and other fields of human diseases. I come from a Biology background and found my way into Bioinformatics through several courses undertaken in my Master's. From there on I saw my future in the computational aspects of Biology and not within a lab.

My mostly biological education during my studies was followed by a Computational Biology PhD at the Beatson Cancer Institute of University of Glasgow UK. This PhD was funded by the European Union under the Marie-Curie Fellowship (Horizon 2020), which enabled me to go on multiple seminars, courses and internships.

As the Bioinformatics world is vast and my passion for it is almost endless, I am constantly trying to increase my knowledge about programming languages, bioinformatic algorithms, or statistical models. There is always something wonderful to discover.
